You are welcome to pick from any of the following topics, but you are strongly encouraged to pick a topic of your own. If you pick your own topic, let me know what it is ahead of time so that we can strategize about it.

Mathematical subjects

Here are several ideas for new mathematical topics you could explore.

Real analysis

Calculus

Did you know that there exist functions that are continuous but which cannot be differentiated anywhere? In some precise way, most (!!) functions have this property!

Did you know that there are continuous functions whose derivative is $0$ "almost everywhere" but which somehow increase from $f(0)=0$ to $f(1)=1$?

Did you know there's a shape that has finite volume but infinite surface area? That means that you can fill the shape with a finite amount of paint, but it requires an infinite amount of paint to cover the surface!

Real analysis

As we saw in class, analysis hinges on the idea of definitions that start with "Let ${\epsilon > 0}$..."

Give examples of analysis-style proofs to rigorously prove results about limits, derivatives, and integrals. You might use the definition of a limit to prove that \( \displaystyle\lim_{x \to 0} \sin(1/x) \) does not exist.
