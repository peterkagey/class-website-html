Pigeonhole principle

The pigeonhole principle says that if you have $n$ holes and $n+1$ pigeons living in the holes, then at least one hole has two or more pigeons.

Moreover, if you have $n$ holes and $>kn$ pigeons, then at least one hole has $k$ or more pigeons.

Problem 1

Sum from subset

Given any five numbers from the set \(\{1, 2, 3, \dots, 8\}\), two of the chosen numbers will add up to \(9\).

Jay Cummings, Proofs: A Long-Form Mathematics Textbook

Problem 2

Midpoints of lattice points

A lattice point in the plane is a point \((x,y)\) such that both \(x\) and \(y\) are integers. Find the smallest number \(n\) such that given \(n\) lattice points in the plane, there exist two whose midpoint is also a lattice point.

Mike Roth, Putnam Pigeonhole Principle

Problem 3

Avoiding divisors

Prove that given any \(101\) integers from \(\{1, 2, 3, \dots, 200\}\), at least one of these numbers will divide another.

Jay Cummings, Proofs: A Long-Form Mathematics Textbook

Problem 4.

Points in simplices

  • Prove that given any five points in a regular equilateral triangle with side lengths of \(1\), there are at least two points whose distance is \(\leq 1/2\).
  • Prove that given any six points in a regular tetrahedron with side lengths of \(\sqrt{2}\), there are at least two points distance of \(\leq 1\).

Problem 5.

Icosahedral face-labelings

Recall that a regular icosahedron is a convex polyhedron having \(12\) vertices and \(20\) faces; the faces are congruent equilateral triangles. On each face of a regular icosahedron is written a nonnegative integer such that the sum of all \(20\) integers is \(39\). Show that there are two faces that share a vertex and have the same integer written on them.

2013 Putnam Exam, Problem A1
