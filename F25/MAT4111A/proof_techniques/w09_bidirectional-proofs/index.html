Bidirectional proofs

Here we will try to optimize something or characterize something. We need to show two directions: everything that we've found works, and everything that works is of the form we found.

See Section 6 of Evan Chen's The OTIS Excerpts.

Problem 0

A holey chessboard

Prove that for all $2^n \times 2^n$ chessboards with a single square removed, it is possible partition the remaining squares of the chessboard into connected components of three squares, where the squares do not all fall on a line.

Problem 1

A holier chessboard

Let $n > 1$ be an odd integer. On an $n \times n,$ chessboard the center square and four corners are deleted. We wish to group the remaining $n^2 - 5$ squares into diagonally adjacent pairs. For which values of $n$ is this possible?

HMMT February 2016, Team #4

Problem 2

Multiplying digits

Lucía multiplies some positive one-digit numbers (not necessarily distinct) and obtains a number $n$ greater than $10$. Then, she multiplies all the digits of $n$ and obtains an odd number. Find all possible values of the units digit of $n$.

Pan-American Girls MO 2021, Problem 4

Proof

Proof sketch

We claim that the units digit must be $5$, which occurs when $n = 3(5) = 15$.

First, the units digit cannot be even, because otherwise the product of the digits would be even.

Next we assume for the sake of contradiction that the units digit is $\{1, 3, 7, 9\}$. Since all integers with $n$ as a factor end with $5$ or $0$, $5$ does not occur as a one-digit number. Similarly, no even numbers can occur in the product. Thus the one-digit numbers must be in $\{1, 3, 7, 9\}$, and so must be of the form $n = 3^k 7^\ell$, since $9 = 3^2$. We claim that all such numbers have an even value in the tens place (and thus are of the form $20a + b$ where $b \in \\{1, 3, 7, 9\\}$), proceeding by induction on $k + \ell$.

When $k + \ell = 1$, $n = 0(10) + 3$ or $n = 0(10) + 7$.

Assume that $n = 3^k 7^\ell = 20a + b$ where $b \in \{1, 3, 7, 9\}$ for all $k + \ell < N$.

Then either $$\begin{align*}3^{k+1} 7^{\ell} &\in \{20(3a) + 3, 20(3a) + 9, 20(3a+1) + 1, 20(3a+1) + 7\} \\ 3^k 7^{\ell + 1} &\in \{20(7a) + 1, 20(7a + 1) + 1, 20(7a + 2) + 9, 20(7a + 3) + 3\}.\end{align*}$$

In either case, the tens place is even, so by the principle of mathematical induction, $n$ cannot be the number Lucía multiplied, a contradiction. Thus the units digit of $n$ must be $5$.

Problem 3

Subset sum

Find, with proof, the least integer $N$ such that if any $2016$ elements are removed from the set $\{1,2,\dots,N\}$, one can still find $2016$ distinct numbers among the remaining elements with sum $N$.

USAJMO 2016

Problem 4

Interval averages

Determine, with proof, if it is possible to arrange $1, 2, \dots, 1000$ in a row such that the average of any pair of distinct numbers is not located between two numbers.

Titu Andreescu and Zuming Feng, 102 Combinatorial Problems, Problem #36

Problem 5

A functional equation

Find all functions $f \colon \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ such that \[f(f(x)^2 + f(y)) = xf(x) + y\] for all $x, y \in \mathbb{R}$.

Kyrgyzstan Olympiad 2012
