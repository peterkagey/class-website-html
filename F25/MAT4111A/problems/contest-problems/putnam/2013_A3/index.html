Suppose that the real numbers $a_0, a_1, \dots, a_n$ and $x$, with $0 < x < 1$, satisfy $$\frac{a_0}{1-x} + \frac{a_1}{1-x^2} + \cdots + \frac{a_n}{1 - x^{n+1}} = 0.$$ Prove that there exists a real number $y$ with $0 < y < 1$ such that $$a_0 + a_1 y + \cdots + a_n y^n = 0.$$

2013 Putnam Exam, Problem A3
