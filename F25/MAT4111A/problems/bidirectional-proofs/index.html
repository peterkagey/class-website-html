Bidirectional proofs

Here we will try to optimize something or characterize something. We need to show two directions: everything that we've found works, and everything that works is of the form we found.

See Section 6 of Evan Chen's The OTIS Excerpts.

Problem 0

Prove that for all $2^n \times 2^n$ chessboards with a single square removed, it is possible partition the remaining squares of the chessboard into connected components of three squares, where the squares do not all fall on a line.

Problem 1

HMMT February 2016, Team #4

Let $n > 1$ be an odd integer. On an $n \times n,$ chessboard the center square and four corners are deleted. We wish to group the remaining $n^2 - 5$ squares into diagonally adjacent pairs. For which values of $n$ is this possible?

Luc√≠a multiplies some positive one-digit numbers (not necessarily distinct) and obtains a number $n$ greater than $10$. Then, she multiplies all the digits of $n$ and obtains an odd number. Find all possible values of the units digit of $n$.

Problem 3

USAJMO 2016

Find, with proof, the least integer $N$ such that if any $2016$ elements are removed from the set ${1,2,\dots,N}$, one can still find $2016$ distinct numbers among the remaining elements with sum $N$.

Problem 4

102 Combinatorial Problems by Titu Andreescu and Zuming Feng, #36

Determine, with proof, if it is possible to arrange $1, 2, \dots, 1000$ in a row such that the average of any pair of distinct numbers is not located between two numbers.

Problem 5

Kyrgyzstan Olympiad 2012

Find all functions $f \colon \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ such that \[f(f(x)^2 + f(y)) = xf(x) + y\] for all $x, y \in \mathbb{R}$.
