## Write-up steps
  1. Choose a problem from a previous Putnam exam or other source.1
  2. Work on the solution for at least 15 minutes or until you've solved it.
  3. Look up a solution to the problem whether or not you've solved it.
  4. Write up a solution in your own words.2
  5. Post your solution to our class Discord channel #math-4111a-solutions.3
  6. Post a link to an existing solution in the channel #math-4111a-solutions.
  7. Post a brief reflection on the problem-solving and write-up process. Did you make any interesting observations or connections to other problems? Would you recommend the problem to your classmates?
  8. Bonus points: if no solution to the problem exists on YouTube, upload a video tutorial of how to solve the problem. (This will count for two of these problem solutions!)
  1. You are encouraged to pick a problem that you did outside of class, but you are also permitted to choose a problem from done during class. If possible, try to post a solution that no other student has posted. ⏎
  2. You are encouraged to write your solution using $\LaTeX$, but you are also permitted to write it up on a tablet/paper and include a screenshot or photo. ⏎
  3. If you are writing up your solution via LaTeX, you can either compile it on your own and include a screenshot, or you can get the TeXit Discord bot to compile it for you. If you use the TeXit bot, you're encouraged to direct message it the code first to make sure it knows how to compile it successfully.⏎
