Choice Exercise 4 [6 + ≤12] (Practice/applications)
  1. [6] We will compute the curvature of the the Lissajous curve \[ L(t) = \begin{pmatrix}\sin (4 t)\\\cos (3 t)\end{pmatrix} \] at various points on its domain of $t \in [0,2\pi]$
    1. Estimate the curvature at $t = 0$ by using the fact that the curvature is the reciprocal of the radius of the circle of best fit.
    2. Find $\alpha \in \mathbb{R}$ so that speed of $\gamma(t) = L(\alpha t)$ is 1 at time $t = 0$.
    3. Compute the acceleration of $\gamma(t)$ at $t = 0$, and find the magnitude of the component of the acceleration which is orthogonal to the tangent direction. Is $\gamma$ speeding up, slowing down, or neither at $t = 0$?
    4. What is the curvature of the curve at $t = 0$, and how does it compare to your initial guess?
    5. [2] Use Mathematica or another graphing tool to draw the circle of best fit to the curve at $t = 0$.
  2. In this part, you will describe a "tube" around a curve, as shown in the figure.
    1. [2] Use ParametricPlot to draw the curve \[ \gamma(t) = \begin{pmatrix} 5 - 9 t + 4 t^2 \\ 3 t + 2 t^2 \end{pmatrix} \] for $t \in [0,1]$, and compute the Frenet frame $(\mathbf{T},\mathbf{N})$, where $\mathbf{T}$ is the unit tangent vector, and $\mathbf{N}$ is the unit normal vector (which is $\mathbf{N}$ rotated $+90^\circ$.)
    2. [3] Give an explicit parameterization of the curve that is "northeast" of the tube at a distance of \(\frac12\) and of the curve that is "southwest" of the tube at a distance of \(\frac12\) and of both of the semicircular "end caps".
    3. [1] Draw the tube around the curve using ParametricPlot.
    4. [1] Draw at least ten tubes around the curve of varying "radii" using ParametricPlot. Does anything weird happen when the radius is big?
    5. [1] Guess the length of the Bézier curve, then compute it.
    6. [4] Compute the area of the "tube" containing the Bézier curve. How does it compare to the length of the curve?

Choice Exercise 8 [10] (Maker Studio)
  1. Describe a differentiable, non-self-intersecting path $\mathbf{c}\colon [0,1] \to [0,1] \times [0,1]$ whose image is inside of the unit square.
