[2 + 4 + 2 + 2 + 4] Choice Exercise 4

Do any subset of the following, but for each part you pick, do the entire part.

In these problems, we will think about how to embed a tetrahedra in $\mathbb{R}^3$. On the previous homework you were instructed on finding the embedding with vertices at \[ (0,0,0), \qquad (2,0,0), \qquad (1, \sqrt{3},0), \quad \text{and} \quad \Big(1,\frac{\sqrt{3}}3, \frac{2\sqrt{6}}3\Big). \] My favorite embedding is perhaps the one with vertices at \[ (1,1,-1), \quad (1,-1,1), \quad (-1,1,1), \quad (-1,-1,-1), \] because it avoids square roots.

  1. [2] A regular tetrahedron is the unique polyhedron (up to similarity) with four vertices with the property that the distance of each edge (between each pair of vertices is the same). In the polyhedron with vertices at \[ (1,1,-1), \quad (1,-1,1), \quad (-1,1,1), \quad (-1,-1,-1), \] where each vertex is connected to every other via an edge, how many edges are there, and how can we see that they all have the same length?
  2. [4] Choose any two non-intersecting edges. There is a unique plane that is equidistant between them. Find out where this plane intersects the other edges. What can you say about these points? (In addition to the four points, come swing by my office and I'll give you a gift of a 3D-printed puzzle that relates to this.)
  3. [2] Starting with the tetrahedron with coordinates of $(1,1,-1)$, $(1,-1,1)$, $(-1,1,1)$, and $(-1,-1,-1)$, compute the position of the vertex that appears when we glue a tetrahedron to one of the faces, as illustrated in the rotatable model below.
  4. [2] Compute dihedral angle (angle between faces) of the tetrahedron. Why can't regular tetrahedra tile space? What's the angle of the gap in the following quadaugmented_tetrahedron, which you can click and drag below.
  5. [4] Find explicit coordinates for the vertex coordinates of the quadaugmented_tetrahedron (which you can click and drag above), where the "central" tetrahedron has coordinates $(1,1,-1)$, $(1,-1,1)$, $(-1,1,1)$, and $(-1,-1,-1)$.
